Other Stuff I do:

Ling Hun is a 2D Platformer Game were Jumping is not affected by gravity (that's what happens when you got your soul stolen). Master the jump to succeed in every level. Enjoy the classic Pixel Art with a mix of Asian ink style in the Background. Change the pace of the game to adapt it to your gamestyle.

With this idea as a premise, we have a simplistic game but with which we can manage an organic difficulty curve by introducing game mechanics progressively and accentuate the difficulty or regulate it thanks to the level design. So we have a game that can be balanced using two completely separate subjects but at the same time connected to achieve a symbiosis that affects the gameplay and its engagement in a direct and organic way.

My participation in Ling Hun began from the very conception of the title. As the idea was based on making a portfolio, I wanted something simple but with a little twist, something that would show that with few mechanics you can achieve a good engagement, a good difficulty curve and squeeze the most out of my skills in the game design and level design sections apart from other sections in which I also made my contributions. For example, when creating the SFX and the soundtrack of the game itself. I also created some pixel art assets, had to animate others and designed all the menus, so I also developed the UI/UX with the help of a flowchart to keep things consistent at all times.

My goal in Ling Hun was to achieve a relaxed experience that could also offer satisfying challenges for practically any type of player, which is why one of the bases of the game is the fact that there are no times, enemies or lives..
